Number Theory

A prime number is a whole number greater than one that has exactly two factors: one and itself.

Factors are numbers that divide evenly into another number without leaving a remainder.

The greatest common factor is the largest number that divides evenly into two or more numbers.

Multiples are numbers produced by multiplying a given number by positive integers.

An integer is any whole number, including positive numbers, negative numbers, and zero.

Rational numbers are numbers that can be expressed as a fraction of two integers.

Irrational numbers are numbers that cannot be expressed as a simple fraction.
