The Novel as a Form

A novel is an extended work of prose fiction exploring complex characters and themes.

The setting is the time, place, and environment in which a story takes place.

A round character is a fully developed character with complex motivations and personality traits.

A flat character is a simple, one-dimensional character with few distinguishing traits.

The point of view is the narrative perspective from which a story is told.

Stream of consciousness is a writing technique depicting the continuous flow of a character's thoughts.

Dialect is the distinctive variety of language used by a particular group or region.
