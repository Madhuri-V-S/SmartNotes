Writing Techniques and Style

Diction is the choice and arrangement of words to achieve a particular style or effect.

Syntax is the arrangement of words and phrases to create grammatically correct sentences.

A simile is a figure of speech comparing two unlike things using the words like or as.

Tone is the author's attitude toward the subject matter as conveyed through word choice.

Juxtaposition is placing contrasting ideas or images side by side to highlight their differences.

An anecdote is a short personal story used to illustrate a point or engage the reader.

Parallelism is the use of grammatically similar structures to create balance and rhythm.
