The American Revolution

The American Revolution was a political uprising where thirteen colonies broke from British rule to form the United States.

The Declaration of Independence was a document proclaiming the colonies separation from Britain in 1776.

Taxation without representation: the colonists' grievance that Britain imposed taxes without colonial consent.

The Continental Congress was the governing body of the American colonies during the Revolution.

Loyalists were colonists who remained loyal to the British Crown during the Revolution.

The Treaty of Paris was the 1783 agreement that formally ended the American Revolution.

Democracy is a system of government where power is held by elected representatives of the people.
