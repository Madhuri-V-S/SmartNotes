Rhetoric and Persuasion

Rhetoric is the art of effective or persuasive speaking and writing.

Ethos is an appeal to credibility or character to convince an audience.

Pathos: an appeal to the emotions of the audience to create persuasive effect.

Logos is an appeal to logic and reason using facts and evidence.

A thesis is the central argument or main claim of an essay or speech.

Hyperbole: deliberate exaggeration used for emphasis or comic effect.

Irony is a contrast between expectation and reality, used for emphasis or humor.
