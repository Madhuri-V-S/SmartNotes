Epic Poetry and Mythology

An epic is a long narrative poem celebrating heroic deeds and significant cultural events.

A myth is a traditional story explaining natural phenomena, cultural practices, or human origins.

The hero's journey is a narrative pattern where a protagonist ventures, faces trials, and returns transformed.

Hubris is excessive pride or arrogance in a character that leads to their downfall.

An allegory is a narrative where characters and events represent abstract ideas or moral qualities.

The chorus was a group of performers in ancient Greek drama who commented on the action.

A deity is a god or goddess worshipped in mythology and religious traditions.
