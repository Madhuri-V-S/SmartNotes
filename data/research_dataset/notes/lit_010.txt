Comedy and Satire

Comedy is a genre of literature intended to amuse through humor, wit, and happy endings.

Satire is a literary technique using humor and irony to criticize human vices or social institutions.

Parody is a humorous imitation of a serious work exaggerating its characteristics for comic effect.

The comic relief is humorous content inserted into a serious narrative to provide emotional release.

Wit is the ability to make clever observations or remarks in an intelligent and amusing way.

A farce is a comedic style relying on exaggerated situations and improbable events for humor.

Sarcasm is a sharp, cutting form of irony used to mock or convey contempt.
