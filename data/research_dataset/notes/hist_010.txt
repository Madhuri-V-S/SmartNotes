The Civil Rights Movement

The Civil Rights Movement was a social movement in the United States fighting racial segregation and discrimination.

Segregation was the enforced separation of people based on race in public spaces and institutions.

Civil disobedience is the nonviolent refusal to obey unjust laws as a form of protest.

The Montgomery Bus Boycott was a protest where African Americans refused to use segregated buses.

Integration is the process of ending segregation and bringing different racial groups together.

The Civil Rights Act was a landmark 1964 law prohibiting discrimination based on race, color, or religion.

Activism is taking direct action to achieve social or political change.
