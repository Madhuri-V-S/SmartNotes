Ancient Egypt

Ancient Egypt was a civilization along the Nile River lasting over three thousand years.

The pharaoh was the supreme ruler of ancient Egypt, considered both king and divine deity.

Hieroglyphics were the ancient Egyptian writing system using pictorial symbols to represent words.

The Nile River was the lifeblood of Egyptian civilization, providing water and fertile soil for farming.

Mummification was the process of preserving a dead body for the afterlife through embalming.

Pyramids are massive stone structures built as tombs for pharaohs in ancient Egypt.

Polytheism is the belief in and worship of multiple gods, practiced throughout ancient Egypt.
