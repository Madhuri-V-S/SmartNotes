The Renaissance

The Renaissance was a period of cultural and intellectual revival in Europe from the 14th to 17th centuries.

Humanism was an intellectual movement emphasizing human potential, reason, and classical learning.

The printing press was an invention by Gutenberg that enabled mass production of books and spread of ideas.

Secularism is the principle of separating religion from other aspects of public life and thought.

Perspective: an artistic technique creating the illusion of depth and three dimensions on a flat surface.

Patronage was the system where wealthy individuals funded artists and scholars during the Renaissance.

The Scientific Revolution was a period of rapid advances in science that challenged traditional views.
