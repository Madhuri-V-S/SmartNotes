Modernist Literature

Modernism was a literary movement rejecting traditional forms and exploring subjective experience.

Fragmentation is a modernist technique using disconnected narrative pieces to reflect disillusionment.

An unreliable narrator is a storyteller whose credibility is compromised by bias or limited knowledge.

Ambiguity is the quality of having multiple possible interpretations or meanings in a text.

Intertextuality is the relationship between texts through references, allusions, or shared themes.

Existentialism is a philosophical theme exploring individual freedom and the search for meaning.

The antiheroes is a protagonist who lacks conventional heroic qualities but drives the narrative.
