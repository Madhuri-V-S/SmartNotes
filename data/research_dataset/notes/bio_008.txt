Human Body Systems

The circulatory system is the network of organs and vessels that transport blood throughout the body.

Neurons are specialized cells that transmit electrical signals throughout the nervous system.

The respiratory system is the organ system responsible for gas exchange, taking in oxygen and releasing carbon dioxide.

Homeostasis is the process by which organisms maintain stable internal conditions despite external changes.

The immune system is the body's defense network against pathogens and foreign substances.

Enzymes are biological catalysts that speed up chemical reactions in the body without being consumed.

Hormones are chemical messengers produced by glands that regulate various body functions.
