Trigonometry

Trigonometry is the branch of mathematics studying relationships between angles and sides of triangles.

The sine of an angle is the ratio of the opposite side to the hypotenuse in a right triangle.

The cosine is the ratio of the adjacent side to the hypotenuse in a right triangle.

Tangent is the ratio of the opposite side to the adjacent side in a right triangle.

A radian is a unit of angular measurement equal to the angle subtended by an arc equal to the radius.

The unit circle is a circle with radius one centered at the origin used to define trigonometric functions.

Amplitude is the maximum displacement from the midline in a periodic function.
