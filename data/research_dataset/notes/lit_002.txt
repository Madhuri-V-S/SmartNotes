Poetry Analysis

A sonnet is a 14-line poem written in iambic pentameter, often exploring themes of love.

Iambic pentameter: a rhythmic pattern with five pairs of unstressed/stressed syllables per line.

Alliteration is the repetition of the same consonant sound at the beginning of nearby words.

The stanza is a grouped set of lines in a poem, separated from other groups by blank lines.

Rhyme scheme: the pattern of rhymes at the end of lines in a poem.

Personification is a figure of speech giving human qualities to non-human things.

An ode is a type of lyric poem that expresses strong feelings about a particular subject.
